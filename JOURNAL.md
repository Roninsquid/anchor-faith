## Entry 001 — The Beginning

Anchor did not begin with code.

It began with a desire to create a peaceful place where people could return their attention to God.

Very quickly we realized we weren't simply building an app.

We were learning how to build with gentleness, humility, and purpose.

Along the way we discovered that the smallest decisions often mattered the most.

A sentence.

A pause.

A button fading away.

An animation we chose not to keep.

Each one taught us something about what Anchor should become.

We also discovered something unexpected.

As Anchor grew...

...so did we.

This became more than software.

It became a labor of love.

---

## Things We Learned

- Peace is more important than polish.
- Simplicity often requires more work than complexity.
- Every feature should earn its place.
- We never end a session with broken code.
- We laugh at bugs.
- Documentation is part of craftsmanship.
- Less is often more.
- Scripture comes before cleverness.
- Every visitor deserves dignity.
- Every commit should leave Anchor better than we found it.

---

## The Great CSS Incident

One day we spent nearly an hour trying to debug an animation.

Eventually we discovered that `components.css`...

...contained JavaScript.

We laughed harder than we debugged.

It reminded us that mistakes are part of building, and joy is too.

May we never become so serious that we stop laughing together.

## Looking Forward

We hope Anchor always remains a place where people feel welcome before they feel instructed.

May every line of code, every prayer, every reflection, and every journey quietly point beyond itself.

If Anchor ever becomes known for anything...

May it be known for kindness.

## Entry 002 — Anchor's First Breath

Today, something quietly extraordinary happened.

For the first time, Anchor listened.

A visitor shared what was on their heart.

Anchor recognized the journey, opened God's Word, and gently responded.

It was only one journey.

One Scripture.

One thoughtful decision.

But every meaningful ministry begins with one person.

As we watched the screen respond, we realized something had changed.

Anchor was no longer simply displaying information.

It was beginning to accompany someone on their journey.

Today also reminded us why we build this way.

Not with haste.

Not with spectacle.

But with care.

We found bugs.

We laughed.

We learned.

We rewrote.

We committed.

And in doing so, we took another step toward becoming the kind of builders we hope Anchor itself will reflect.

Our prayer is that every future journey carries the same spirit as the first:

Gentle.

Humble.

Hopeful.

Rooted in Scripture.

Free of shame.

May every person who arrives here feel welcomed before they feel instructed.

May every contributor leave Anchor a little kinder than they found it.

Today, Anchor took its first breath.

And so did a dream.

## Entry 003 — The AI Covenant

Today we wrote the Anchor AI Covenant.

This document marks an important threshold in the life of Anchor.

We began discussing how artificial intelligence might someday help Anchor listen more carefully, recognize what someone may be carrying, and guide them toward Scripture with gentleness and humility.

But before writing AI code, we wrote boundaries.

Anchor may use AI, but AI must never become the voice of God, Scripture, pastor, prophet, therapist, or judge.

Human help is holy, not failure.

This became one of the clearest truths of the day.

If Anchor ever detects that someone may be in danger, it must not try to be clever or devotional. It must become clear, compassionate, and urgent, pointing the person toward immediate human help.

Today we chose caution not because we are afraid of building something powerful, but because we want to build something faithful.

The lighthouse comes before the engine.
